How to Cook

April 16, 2020 2-minute read

Note for future readers: This post was made during the Great Plague of 2020. However, most of it should still be applicable.

Alright, so you’re locked up in quarantine, your DoorDash budget is running low and your intestines are trashed from too much ramen.

I wish I knew how to cook :(

I hear you say. No worries fam, I gotchu.

I’m a strong believer in learning from fundamentals so I’m not gonna give you a list of Top 10 easiest recipies. Instead I’m gonna be teaching you how to make a tasty meal with whatever you got in your pantry, simply by knowing how to cook.

Things that make up a good meal:

  1. Flavour
  2. Technique
  3. Nutrition
  4. Ingredients
  5. Presentation

Flavour

Right, since you’re probably a college kid you aren’t looking for subtle floral notes or hints of truffle or whatever pretentious sounding crap is popular these days. Simple, bold and well balanced flavours are all you need to get your fix of that sweet, sweet dopamine.

Here are the 5 basic flavours in cooking:

  • Salt: Found too much in processed food, too little in home cooked meals. If your food lacks a certain “something” this is probably it.
  • Sweet: Ah, the ever demonized sugar.
  • Sour
  • Bitter
  • Spice
  • Umami
