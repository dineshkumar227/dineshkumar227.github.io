The Ethics of Dog Ownership

May 17, 2020 1-minute read

I think dogs are one of the cruelest things humanity has done. We’ve taken the majestic, independent wolf and genetically engineered to become our friend-slave. Well, that’s the fate of the lucky ones. Some dogs are trained for a lifetime of servitude of labour (K9 and Sled Dogs), some are merely vessels of narcissism of their owners, being forced to learn “tricks” and other useless bullshit in order to compete in dick measuring contests with other narcissists.

A common, and valid response is that dogs love to be pets and companions. Learning tricks and pleasing their owners does give dogs pleasure. However, I’d argue that this is nothing more than genetically engineered Stockholm Syndrome. Whenever a dog doesn’t show these traits, it gets termed as “difficult” or “rabid” and gets sent to shelters to be murdered. Sometimes dogs are murdered just because they’re not cute enough to be adopted.

The worst part of all this we can’t simply let our pets out into the wild. Most dogs are completely dependent on their owners and would not survive a month in the wild. Even if they could, our cities are not built to handle dog overpopulation from a bunch of unneutered dogs running about.
